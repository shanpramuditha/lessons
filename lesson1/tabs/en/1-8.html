vegetables


  • Green leafy vegetables are rich in beta-carotene vitamin C and folic acid. They are also rich in calcium, iron, fiber and antioxidants.

  • Dark green leaves (spinach) have more vitamin A than light- coloured ones.

  • All vegetables are rich sources of vitamin C, vitamin B, soluble fiber and antioxidants.

  • Generally low fat and protein.

Fruits


  •       Important source of vitamin C, beta-carotene and fiber.

  • They also contain less fat and protein.

  •       Yellow/orange coloured fruits such as mango, papaya, pineapple and laulu are good sources of beta- carotene.

  •       Fruits like avocado are rich in monounsaturated fats and are good for heart health.

  •       Most fruits are rich in antioxidants.

Milk and dairy products


  • Excellent sources of protein, energy, calcium, vitamin A and riboflavin.

    E.g. yoghurt, curd, cheese, butter
