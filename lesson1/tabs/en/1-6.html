The six major classes of nutrients


Nutrients in food belong for six major categories:

  • Carbohydrates
  • Fats
  • Proteins
  • Vitamins
  • Minerals
  • Water
In addition, plant food contains dietary fiber which is not a nutrient but is essential for our good health. Although strictly speaking, fiber is not a nutrient, it acts almost like one.

Type of food nutrients


Depending on the quantity that we have to eat every day, nutrients in food are divided into two categories called macro and micronutrients.

  • Macronutrients- needed in large quantities every day (tens or hundreds of grams) E.g. carbohydrates, proteins, fats, oils, water

  • Micronutrients- needed in minute quantities (micrograms or milligrams) in our daily diet, but are vital for health. E.g. vitamins and minerals.
