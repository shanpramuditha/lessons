BMI-for-age


BMI is used differently for children. It is calculated the same way as for adults, but then compared to typical values for other children of the same age.

A BMI that is less than the 5th percentile is considered underweight and above the 95th percentile is considered to be obese. BMI between the 85th and 95th percentile are considered to be overweight.
