Internationally accepted BMI classification for adults


According to the internationally accepted BMI classification suggested by the World Health Organization (WHO, 1993), the ideal BMI range for adults is between 18.5 â€“24.9. Either side of this range is undesirable as it indicates under-nutrition (<18.5) or over-nutrition (>24.9).

In particular, when BMI is 30 or above, the person is considered obese and at high risk for contracting non communicable diseases like hypertension, type II diabetes, heart disease and some cancers. However, there is increasing evidence of a high prevalence of type II diabetes and increased cardiovascular risk factors in Asia, where the average BMI is below the cut- off point 25kg/m2.
