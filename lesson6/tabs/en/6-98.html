Growth during infancy and early childhood


Growth during infancy and childhood depends on birth weight, adequacy of infant feeding and absence of infection.

Available data clearly indicates that exclusively breast-fed infants thrive normally during the first six month of life and have fewer illnesses (diarrhea, respiratory tract infection and fever) than those receiving supplements in addition to breast ild. steps should be taken for the protection and promotion of the practice of breast-feeding.

Introduction of mild substitutes too early and introduction of complimentary food too late are associated with increased risk of under nutrition and infection among infants.

Nutrition of women of child bearing age


The diet of a woman before and during pregnancy has an immense influence on the course of pregnancy and health outcome, both after delivery and in the future. good maternal nutrition is essential to help support a successful pregnancy and breast- feeding. Women planning a pregnancy should follow a balanced nutrient-rich diet. care should be taken to monitor the nutritional status of vulnerable groups (including adolescent girls) to ensure a healthy pregnancy outcome.
