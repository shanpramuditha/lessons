Bod Mass Index (BMI)


The body mass index (BMI), or Quetelet Index, is a measure which compares a person’s weight and height. Though it does not actually measure the percentage of body fat, it is used to estimate a healthy body weight based on a person’s height. Due to its ease of measurement and calculation, it is most widely used diagnostic tool to identify weight problems within a population. i.e. usually, whether individuals are underweight, overweight or obese.

BMI is just a guide; it does not apply to elderly populations, pregnant women or athletes.

BMI can also be determined using a BMI chart, which displays BMI as a function of weight (horizontal axis) and height (vertial axis) BMI or colours for different BMI categories.
