Growth during infancy and early childhood

Nutrition and growth in the womb


Nutrition in the womb is the process that delivers to the foetus what it needs to grow and develop into a healthy baby. a foetus receives its nutrition from two sources. the first source of nutrients is from the mother's diet during pregnancy and the other source is from the mother's own body.

the placenta, which is a part of the baby that attaches it to te womb, captures , nutrients from the mother's blood and transports it to the baby. the growth of the placenta and the food it supplies are the key to health for a lifetime.

Low birth weight (LBW)


low birth weight or birth weight less that 2500g is a major public health problem in many developing countries. about 17% of babies born in Sri Lanka are of LBW as compared to 4% in some developed countries.

Incidence of low birth weight is the highest among low-income groups. Maternal malnutrition and anemia appear to be significant risk factors in its occurrence.
