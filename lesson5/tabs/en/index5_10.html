Nutritional benefits of fermented products

ඇස්කොබික් අම්ලය නැතහොත් විටමින් C

  • Microorganism are not always harmful; many are harmless and some microorganisms are extremely useful too. These special group of microorganisms are used to make fermented products e.g. bread, yoghurt, curd, cheese, vinegar, soy sauce, thosai, idley, natural white hopper mixtures, etc.

  • The benefits of these microorganisms lie in their capability to convert certain components in food into a more palatable form (e.g. curd), enhance flavor (e.g. soy sauce), improve texture (e.g. bread, white hopper), improve nutritive value (e.g. yoghurt) and preserve food (e.g. gherkin).

  • Fermented dairy Products like curd or yoghurt are given to people with lactose intolerance. In general, most Asians have this problem due to lack of enzymes in their digestive system that can digest lactose in fresh milk products digests lactose in fresh milk. Bacteria (e.g. lactobacillus bulgaricus, streptococcus thermophiles) in fermented milk products digests lactose and decreases their availability in fermented products. (e.g. yoghurt).
