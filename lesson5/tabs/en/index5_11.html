Smart cooking tips

Food is healthy if nutrients are preserved during cooking. Cooking tips given below will enhance the nutritive value of your food.


Cooking

  • You may need to cover some of the food while cooking to prevent nutrient loss and retain taste and flavor, but in certain cases, cooking utensils should be kept open e.g. cooking of manioc

  • Stirring and sieving should not be prolonged, because air is incorporated into food which destroy vitamin C

  • Most food should be cooked till it is done because it is only then that it gives its flavor and is digested well (rice, polos, lentils etc.

  • To preserve nutrients, food should not be overcooked

  • Vegetables should retain its colour after cooking

  • Pressure cooking helps retain nutrients

  • Cooked food should not be re-heated more than once

Vegetables and fruits

  • Cut just before you cook, as vitamins are lost upon contact with air

  • Wash vegetables before cutting so that soluble nutrients do not get drained

  • Vegetables should be taken without peeling off skin. Or else, a very thin peel should be removed because many nutrients are found under the skin

  • Vegetables which are not consumed raw may be ideally cooked just before you eat Vegetables which are not consumed raw may be ideally cooked just before you eat

  • Use an abundance of fresh seasonal fruit and vegetables

  • Cut fruit and vegetables into big pieces in order to reduce the surface area coming into contact with air and water

  • Vegetables should be cooked for a minimal time in boiling water so that their colour, taste and nutrients are preserved

  • Salads should be prepared just before serving

  • Avoid buying pre-cut vegetables

Oils

  • Coconut oil is an acceptable cooking oil

  • Use fresh coconut oil that doesn’t have any off/rancid smell for cooking purposes

  • Do not re-heat the same oil that was once used

Residues of food

  • The water in which pulses have been soaked for a long time must be drained, as anti-nutritional factors like phytates, soluble in water, may be present

  • The water in which rice is cooked should not be thrown away as it may contain vitamin B. you should add the correct amount of water and not strain water after cooking rice.

Use of acid food

  • Lime juice, tomatoes, vinegar or yoghurt as dressings in salads prevents loss of vitamin C
