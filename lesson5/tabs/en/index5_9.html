Steps to minimize he loss of vitamin C during cooking/ processing

Ascorbic acid (vitamin C)

  • Use less water in washing and cooking.

  • If boiling potatoes, avoid cutting or peeling before boiling.

  • Place vegetables directly in boiling water to keep out oxygen.

  • While cooking, cover food with a lid to reduce access to oxygen.

  • Avoid iron and copper pots for cooking. Iron and copper increases the oxidation of vitamin C

  • Avoid sodium bicarbonate to preserve colour.

  • Use pressure cooking, if available.

  • Keep fruits and vegetables at a low temperature during storage.

Thiamin

  • Highly water-soluble and heat labile. Thiamin is sensitive to heat at neutral and alkaline pH (10-20% loss).

  • Baking Powder and sulphites increase the loss of thiamin. Baking powder is the main leavening agent in certain bakery products and instant hoppers. Hence there is a potential risk to lose thiamin in such products.

Riboflavin

  • Sensitive to sunlight (particularly UV). Nearly 50% of riboflavin will be lost in 2 hours of exposing the food to bright sunlight while 20% will be lost on a dull day.

  • Milk is a good source of riboflavin, thus milk and their products should not be exposed to direct sunlight.

Vitamin B6

  • Meat, green vegetables and cereals with bran are rich sources of vitamin B6, which is sensitive to heat.

  • Processed products may contain lesser amounts of B6 than natural forms.

Folate

  • About 50% is lost after cooking of vegetables. Losses are less in meat and eggs. Processed products may contain lesser amounts of B6 than natural forms.

Vitamin A and Carotenes

  • Not dissolved in cooking water. Stable at mild heat, but destroyed due to light.

Vitamin E

  • Slowly destroyed by heat during frying. Also sensitive to light. Deep fat frying and freezing may also be responsible for losing vitamin E.

Minerals

  • Water soluble minerals may be dissolved in cooking water.

  • Iodine may be lost from marine fish during freezing. In addition, a greater amount of iodine is lost if you add iodated salt when the food is hot or keep an iodated salt container near a fire place or gas cooker. Keep iodated salt in a closed dark container and always away from heat and direct sunlight.

Note to parents

If you have high blood pressure, you should limit eating processed products containing high salt (e.g. cheese, soya sauce, butter, canned fish in brine, savoury snacks etc.)
