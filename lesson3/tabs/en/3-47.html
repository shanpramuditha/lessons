Food allergens

Food allergy can be defined as an adverse immune-mediated reaction to food. Any food can cause an allergic reaction, but the following are the most common source of allergens.
  •       Peanuts
  •       Crustaceans
  •       Milk
  •       Eggs
  •       Fish
  •       Soybeans
  •       Gluten (Wheat, rye and barley)
  •       Tree Nuts (Cashew, almond, walnuts, pecans)
  •       Mustard
  •       Sesame
  •       Celery
  •       Sulphur dioxide and sulphites used in foods as preservatives
Different individuals may be allergic to different types of food. Hence, it is important to identify and understand which food is allergic to you and try to avoid it. If you are aware of potential food allergies, keep your physician informed about it before getting your prescription.
