To prevent / avoid ingesting toxins through food you should:


  • Not eat food contaminated with fungus. Certain fungus can produce toxic chemical compounds (e.g. aflatoxin) in their growing media, so avoid consuming fungal infested peanuts, corn and cake like sweets. Fungal infested food should not be used even as animal feed since there is a potential danger of aflatoxin reaching humans through contaminated animal products, e.g. milk.

  • Not by potatoes with green-colored skin. The green color is due to exposure of tubers to sunlight with the formation of some toxic compounds (solanin).

  • Avoid keeping or serving pickles using aluminum utensils or cutlery. Pickles are good appetizers and effective rice pullers which contain vinegar. Vinegar or diluted acetic acid is a good solvent for aluminum may cause severe chronic health problems.
