What is clean water?


Water which is usually clear and colorless with no visible impurities and smell may be identified as clean water. Even rainwater collected in unpolluted tanks is satisfactory as long as the tanks are protected from contamination from birds and other animals. However, water must be boiled and cooled before drinking.

Water is quite a powerful source of spreading diseases such as cholera, hepatitis, typhoid etc.

Thus, for drinking purposes and cleaning of food and hands, you should always use clean water. Furthermore, use clean water to:


  • Wash fruits and vegetables
  • Add to food
  • Make drinks
  • Make ice
  • Clean cooking and eating utensils
  • Wash hands

Note:


Be careful when you taste ‘ice saruwat’ or ice packets which may be available at a ‘pola’ or at your school gate. If contaminated water is used for preparation of these products, there is a potential risk of water-borne diseases.
If the water supply to your home gives an order it could be chlorine. Please check with your parents.
