Food poisoning


Food poisoning incidents have become a common topic in day-to-day life more than ever before.

  •       The symptoms of the most common types of food poisoning generally start within a few hours to a few days of eating contaminated food.

  •       Possible symptoms include abdominal cramps, diarrhea, fever, headache, nausea and vomiting.

  • Food poisoning can affect one person or can occur as an outbreak in a group of people who all ate the same contaminated food. Food poisoning tends to occur frequently at picnics, school cafeterias and large social functions.
