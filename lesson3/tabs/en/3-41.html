Natural and induced toxins in food

Food safety cannot be achieved by simply maintaining hygiene and safeguarding contamination. To ensure food safety, it is important to understand potentially hazardous natural toxins in food and also the toxins formed due to poor cooking practices.
So called “natural”food do not always guarantee food safety.

Why?

  • Certain chemicals may be applied during cultivation (E.g. Insecticides/ pesticides applied to vegetables
  • Toxic chemicals may be generated in response to environment (E.g. Green coloured skin formed in potatoes when exposed to sunlight)
  • Harmful chemicals may be present in edible food (E.g. Cassava, pulses, cabbage etc.)
  • Toxin producing microbes may grow in edible plant materials (E.g. Black color fungus in peanut)
