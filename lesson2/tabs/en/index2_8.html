Vitamin A deficiency (VAD)

Vitamin A deficiency is one of the most dangerous nutritional diseases among young children because it damages the eyes and cause blindness and also increases the risk of infection and death. It is regarded as the single, primary cause of preventable childhood blindness in developing countries.

Vitamin A deficiency is a public health problem in Sri Lanka. Its prevalence among children (6 months to 6 years)

Vitamin A is known as an anti- infective vitamin. Children who lack vitamin A are more likely to get gut, respiratory and other infections and die from them easily. Very young children having severe PEM are at an increased risk of VAD

When vitamin A is deficient:

  •       Resistance to infection is decreased. The cells lining the gut and respiratory track secrete less mucus, enabling bacteria to stick to the cells and cause infect ions easily.

  •       Immunity against infection is decreased. The white blood cells cannot fight infections so well.

Importance of vitamin A

  •       For growth and development

  •       To provide immunity

  •       To maintain healthy vision

  •       For healthy skin and hair

  •       For normal cell division and differentiation

  •       Has antioxidant properties

  •       For healthy reproduction

Signs of vitamin A deficiency

  •       Low blood levels of vitamin A (retinol)
  •       Night blindness
  •       Botot’s spots in the eye

      A severe deficiency state is characterized by xerophthalmia (dry eye), which is the most easily recognized clinical sign of the deficiency.

How to correct vitamin A deficiency

Increased consumption of vitamin A rich animal and plant products may provide a food–based solution to the problem. E.g. yellow and orange coloured fruits (mango, papaw, water melon etc.) and vegetables (carrot, pumpkin, sweet potato, tomato etc.), dark green leafy vegetables, liver, milk and eggs. Mega dose vitamin A capsules are used to treat the deficiency.

Note:

Food should contain some oil to help absorption of vitamin A from plant materials. For example, adding coconut when preparing ‘sambols’ or ‘mallum’

Brest milk is a very good source of vitamin A. colostrum (first milk) is a rich source of vitamin A received by a baby from its mother, hence it should not be discarded.
