Iron deficiency anaemia (IDA)

රAnaemia, which is public health problem in Sri Lanka, is a condition that results when the haemoglobin in the blood is inadequate. When haemoglin getting oxygen to the cells.
  • Pre-school children and pregnant women are highly susceptible to IDA
  • Iron deficiency and/or vitamin B12 deficiency may also be responsible.
  • Severe anaemia in pregnant women increase the risk of maternal and fetal death.mild anaemia may increase low birth weight deliveries. Anaemia reduces people’s ability to work and increase susceptibility to infections.

(මුලාශ්‍ර : වෛද්‍ය පර්යේෂණ ආයතනය)

Signs of anaemia

ළදරු, ළමා, තරුණ වැඩිහිටි යන සියළු පාර්ශවයන්ට ප්‍රෝටීන් ශක්ති ඌණතාවය අහිතකර අයුරින් බලපෑම් ඇති කරයි.
  • Paleness of the tongue and inside the lips

  • Headache/ dizziness

  • Lack of energy or fatigue (tiring easily)

  • Loss of appetite

  • Fast and irregular heartbeat

  • Swelling in the legs

  • Brittle nails

  • Poor memory and learning abilities

How to correct iron deficiency anaemia

Absorption od iron from food is influenced by multiple factors. One important factor is the form of iron. Heme Iron, found in animal sources (fish, meat, liver, poultry), is highly available for absorption. Non- heme iron, found in vegetables sources and also in cow’s milk and eggs, is less available.

The absorption of non-heme iron can be improved when a source of heme iron is consumed in the same meal. In addition, iron absorption- enhancing food (fish, meat, poultry and vitamin C rich food like orange, tomato etc. can also increase the absorption, some can inhibit or interfere with iron absorption (consuming tea and coffee immediately after meals)

Iron deficiency can also be corrected by:

  •       Increasing bioavailable iron in the diet by using iron fortified food.
  •       Increasing consumption of iron and folate rich food (E.g. liver, spinach, lentils, okra, cabbage, banana, avocado, green gram, kurakkan etc.). however, these plant food may contain absorption inhibitors ( fiber and polyphenols) or iron absorption promoters (vitamin C)
  •       Controlling parasites (E.g. hookworm, whipworm) and other diseases affecting the loss of red blood cells (E.g. malaria, thalassemia)
  •       Using iron supplements when there is a high iron demand by the body (E.g. during pregnancy)
