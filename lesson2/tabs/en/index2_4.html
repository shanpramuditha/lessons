Consequences of protein-energy malnutrition

There are physical signs, learning signs and other behavioural signs associated with PEM such as:

  • Increased child mortality
  • Low physical development
  • Low mental development
  • Increased susceptibility to infections/frequent sickness
  • Reduce work capacity
  • Reduced productive life span
  • Early deaths prior to retirement age
  • enmia in pregnant women may increase low birth weight deliveries

How to correct protein-energy malnutrition

  •       Provide food which is energy-rich and has good quality protein. (E.g. meat, fish, eggs, milk etc.)
  •       Increase the energy content of food by adding oil, nuts etc. (E.g. fried rice)
  •       Increase the number of servings to 4-5 times a day
  •       Provide enough fruits and vegetables to get vitamin A, which improves immunity
  •       Treat infections quickly
  •       Continuously monitor growth (weight and height)
  •       Mothers with babies having PEM at breast feeding stage should continue breast feeding
