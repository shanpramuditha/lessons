NUTRITION RELATED HEALTH ISSUES

OBJECTIVE

to increase awareness of diet-related health problems such as under-nutrition, over-nutrition (obesity) and other diet related non- communicable diseases prevalent in our country.

Learning outcomes

At the end of the module students will be able to:


  • Identify factors affecting under/ over nutrition and other nutrition related non-communicable diseases, their signs and symptoms.

  • Describe the importance of having a good nutritional status for a healthy and productive life.

  • Identify correct dietary practices to prevent nutrition related health problems.

Overview

We are aware that a balanced diet is required to grow, be healthy and active. If you do not obtain macro and micronutrients in their required quantities, it may cause health complications in the long-term.

Malnutrition may occur as a result of various factors. Food insecurity or low food availability is one of the main reasons from malnutrition. Inadequate child care also affects the nutritional status of children. Weaning food with poor nutritional value, due to mothers’ lack of knowledge of the nutritional needs of children, has been identified as one of the major reasons for poor nutritional status in children.Furthermore, unavailability of clean water and poor sanitation in the household and time for child care are some other reasons related to infant and child malnutrition in the community.

In addition to the prevalence of under-nutrition, many developing countries in the world today are faced with diet-related non-communicable diseases (DR-NCDs) such as diabetes, hypertension, and heart diseases. A holistic approach, comprising improvements in the social-economic environment together with dietary and behavioural modifications inculcated through nutrition education is required to control this dual burden of nutrition- related health problems existing in the community.
