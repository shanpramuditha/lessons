Major under- nutrition problems in Sri Lanka

Inadequate intake of nutrient rich food together with frequent infections have been identified as the immediate cause of infant and child under-nutrition. The most common under-nutrition problems prevalent in Sri Lanka are the following:

  • Protein-Energy Malnutrition (PEM)
  • Iron Deficiency Anemia (IDA)
  • Vitamin A deficiency ( VAD)
  • Iodine Deficiency Disorders (IDD)

Protein- Energy Malnutrition (PEM)

PEM develops when the consumption of protein and energy are insufficient to satisfy the body’s nutritional needs. This may also occur in people who are unable to absorb vital nutrients or convert them to energy. PEM causes a range of conditions which differ in signs and severity. An undernourished child may be low in weight and height and look thin and/or small in comparison to the average of properly nourished children.

Indicators of PEM

  • Underweight (low weight for age)

  • Stunting (low height for age)

  • Wasting (low weight for height)
